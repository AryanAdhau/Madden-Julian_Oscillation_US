This repository contains the analysis workflow, datasets, and visualization scripts used to investigate how the Madden–Julian Oscillation (MJO) influences storm activity, coastal flooding potential, and Total Water Levels (TWL) along the United States Atlantic and Gulf coastlines.

Using the Real-time Multivariate MJO (RMM) index Australian Bureau of Meteorology, NOAA CORA coastal reanalysis water levels National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration, tropical cyclone records from the National Hurricane Center, and atmospheric datasets such as Outgoing Longwave Radiation (OLR), this project identifies periods of strongly favorable MJO phases (8–1–2) during Atlantic hurricane seasons and evaluates their relationship with tropical cyclone formation and extreme coastal water levels.

Key Features
Detection of active and strongly favorable MJO events
Linkage of tropical cyclone genesis to MJO phase/amplitude
Coastal TWL anomaly analysis during storm seasons
Spatial hotspot mapping of vulnerable coastlines
Time-series, climatology, and multi-year visualization outputs
Case studies (e.g., 2021 active hurricane season)
Objective

To better understand how intraseasonal tropical variability modulates Atlantic storms and coastal flood hazards, improving climate-risk awareness and forecasting applications.

Tools & Libraries

Python, Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib, Xarray, Plotly, Folium, NetCDF
